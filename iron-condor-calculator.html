What is an Iron Condor?

An Iron Condor is a neutral options strategy where you simultaneously sell an out-of-the-money call spread and an out-of-the-money put spread on the same underlying asset with the same expiration date. It profits when the underlying stays within a defined range.

Iron Condor Payoff Structure

The strategy consists of 4 legs: buy a lower put (Long Put Strike), sell a higher put (Short Put Strike), sell a lower call (Short Call Strike), and buy a higher call (Long Call Strike). Your maximum profit is the net credit received, and your maximum loss is the spread width minus the credit.

📈 Max Profit

Net premium received when the underlying expires between the two short strikes.

📉 Max Loss

Spread width minus net credit, occurring if the underlying moves beyond either wing.

⚖️ Breakeven Points

Two breakeven prices: Short Put Strike minus credit and Short Call Strike plus credit.

🎯 Best For

Sideways markets with low volatility expectations. Generates income in stable conditions.
